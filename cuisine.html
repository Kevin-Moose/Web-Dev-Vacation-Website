Tavë Kosi is a baked dish that contains pieces of lamb meat (or chicken), and rice. It is surrounded by a mixture of yogurt and eggs bring the dish together and creates a very pleasant and comfy taste.

With these in mind you can understand how some dishes on this page may look familiar, and how they could have been altered and changed for the locals in this small country. Check out more dishes below.

Qofte (Cho-fteh) is a traditional Albanian dish related to the Kofta dish that's widespread across the Mediterranean. It is made with various spices, grounded up meat, and fried into crispy balls. It is complimented well with tzaztiki sauce and french fries.

Pastiçe is a baked pasta dish made out of spaghetti, eggs, and feta cheese. It has a crispy and crunchy top, with a soft and sharp taste on the inside. It is very dense, very filling, and very delicious.

Byrek is a dish made with filo dough, and a variety of fillings that can include cheese, spinach, tomatoes, onions, or meat. They can come in many shapes such as triangles, pie shaped, or spiral. They make a very nice breakfast dish as its light, crispy, and nutritous.

Stuffed peppers are some of the most tasties dishes around the world. Albania has a variant of its own that is very astetically pleasing, and tasty. It can be made with any color peppers depending on what kind of taste you're reaching for. The rice is mixed with a variety of meat and spices in order to provide every bite a big "Mmmm" feeling.

Petulla is a fried dough that is usually enjoyed with sprinkled sugar, jam, or honey. It is a fluffy, and airy treat that is both delicious, and a joy to eat.

Trileçe is a traditonaly Albanian dessert inspired from the South American Tres leche. The dessert was reverse-engineered and in its original version included milk from three different animals (cow, goat, and waterbuffalo), as well as having a caramel cream topping. Today it is usually prepared the same way as tres leches but it has kept its famous caramel topping that makes it mouthwatering.
