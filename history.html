The history of Albania begins when the Proto-Indo-European speaking people of 5500 B.C. ventured into Southeastern Europe. Many different group of ethnicities would evolve from the span of 2500 years. The 4 major ones that are important to mention about are the Illyrians, Dacians, Thracians, and Greeks. It is not clear which exact group Albanians evolved from but many theories point to either Illyrian or Thraco-Dacian.

This is supported by the fact that the Modern Albanian language still retains Doric Greek loanwords that were in use during 800-300 B.C by the Greeks. Loanwords are foreign words that enter a different language through interactions between two cultures. Therefore the ancestors of Albanians had to have bordered the Greeks and interacted with each other either by trading, fighting, or intermixing. This leaves the Thracians and Illyrians as the possible predecessor of the Albanians.

This region would often be a major crossroad into Europe for various Empires that would aspire to control it. The majority of Southeastern Europe would fall to the Roman Empire. During these times the Romans heavily influenced and partially assimilated the Illyrians, Thracians, and Dacians.

The Dacians would over time would evolve into modern day Romanians who speak a full Latin language while the Illyrians would share a similar fate with the exception of a few Southern Illyrian tribes who somehow retained their identity but had their culture heavily influenced by the Romans. Many loanwords would replace the native variants and be responsible for over 40-60% of the total loanwords. This is why the language of Albania today has over 2,000 loanwords that are of Latin origin, and at one point was incorrectly thought to have been a Romance language.

As the Roman Empire began to crumble, all of Europe began to have a huge influx of migrations. The Anglo-Saxons would carve Britain, the Goths would take over Rome itself, and the Slavs would cause wide-spread exctinctions of many cultures in the Balkans.

During 500 A.D an influx of Slavic tribes pushed deep into the Balkans. The Northern Illyrians would be assimilated by the Slavs, while the Thracians would be conquered by the Bulgars. This crisis caused any surviving Balkan cultures to push further South in attempt to avoid being decimated. The ancestors of the Albanians would push further South into Greek territory in order to seek land for its refugees.

Although the West Roman Empire would fall, the East would continue to thrive. Over time the Byzantine Empire would flourish and unite the Greeks, Albanians, Anatolians, and even the invading Slavs under one banner through conquests and alliances. One Illyrian tribe that did manage to avoid being assimilated were the Albanoi. This is where the name of Albania is thought to have originated from and lied in modern day central Albania.

Power in the Balkans would often fluctuate, but nothing would prepare the Balkans for the events to come. A new group of invadors would show up to its doors. During the Middle Ages, the Ottoman Turks who originally hailed from Central Asia would carve the Mediterranean world. Piece by piece they would conquer the Byzantine Empire. One of the last regions to fall would be the territory of Albania in which a man known as Gjergj "Skanderbeg" Kastrioti would face an insurmountable opponent.

Kastrioti was a noble man who at a young age was given to the Ottomans as a blood tax known as Devirshime. This tax would require young Christians within Europe to be given to the Turks for education, assimilation, and military conscription. Skanderbeg would ascend the Ottoman ranks and gain the title of Skanderbeg meaning "Lord Alexander" in Turkish.

During one of the Ottoman campaigns however, Gjergj (pronounced "Gehr-gee") would desert the Turks and reclaim the territory of Albania that his father had previously defended. The castle of Kruje would be where he would decide the fate of his future actions.

Over the course of the 30 years the Ottomans would test the strength of the Albanian people, and its allies. Often facing 30,000 to 100,000 soldiers against Skanderbegs 5-10,000 strong army. Time and time again Skanderbeg would use the tactics he learned in the Ottoman army to defeat his opponent. Using feigned retreats, and flanking with light cavalry units he would delivery devastating blows to the Turks. The Turks would sue for peace frequently to prevent losing territory after their defeats.

When not fighting the Turks, Skanderbeg and his forces would be employed by the King of Naples to settle his conflicts, which often proved victorious. Skanderbeg's own family crest would be the inspiration to the national flag of Albania. The crest would be inspired from the identity the Albanians had. Their history with the Roman empires that favored the Eagle, as well as the war flag of the Byzantiums which also sported a double headed eagle.

But just as the cycle of life continues, so did that of Skanderbeg's life. At the age of 62, Gjergj would succumb to Malaria and perish. Leaving behind a country without a leader. Shortly after his fall the Ottoman Turks would take advantage of this tragedy and take over the remainder of Albania. During this period of history the Albanians would undergo a massive amount of change. A large amount of Turkish words would enter the language, many Christians would be converted to Islam, and the country's manpower would be used to fuel the Ottoman armies.

Albanians would be under Ottoman occupation for almost half a century enduring many hardships, and having its populace under constant assimilation. As the Ottoman empire weakened towards modern age, Albanians would constantly revolt trying to gain more autonomy. Upon reaching the year 1912 they would finally gain full control and gain independence after the Balkan wars pushed the Ottoman borders all the way back to Anatolia.

The borders that were drawn by Western powers however, would leave almost 1/3 of Albanians outside of the country's territory. Many wars later, the country would enter a period of Communism that isolated the country from the rest of the world for half a century until its fall in the 90's. Upon this event the country would finally modernize like its neighbors, form connections with countries, and open its borders to the rest of the world.

Albania today has improved tremendously. It has joined the military defense group known as NATO, and has been working towards joining the European Union. It has also attempted to warm relations with its neighbors as well as include themselves in world affairs. Albanians largest trading partner is Italy and Greece. These two countries are also where the majority of the diaspora can be found in, accounting for over a million in Italy, and an estimated half a million or more in Greece.

The biggest issue concering Albania today is Kosovo. A region next to in which 93% of the population identify as Albanians. It is currently contested between Serbia who claims the region is there's and Albania who wishes to unify with their people after a century of seperation. A deadly conflict in the 90's sent shockwaves across Europe and many wounds are left still healing.
